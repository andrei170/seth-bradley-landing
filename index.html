The Aspiring Fund Founder

You are 6-18 months out from your first raise. You have a deal thesis. You do not have the structure mapped, the Reg D path picked, or the investor-doc stack drafted. You want the regulation-grounded version of the conversation before you sit down with bespoke counsel.

02

The Active Syndicator

You have 1-3 deals closed and you are building toward a closed-end fund. You know what you do not know about ongoing compliance, K-1 timing, Reg D switch logic, and the difference between Investment Company Act §3(c)(1) and §3(c)(7). You want structured guidance between bespoke engagements.

The Work

Three pillars. Twelve weeks.

No capital-raise shortcuts. No guru framing. Every module cites the regulations, releases, and case law it is built on. Live each week with Seth, weekly office hours, and a 1:1 deal-structure review at week 8.

Pillar One · Weeks 1-4

Read The Regulations, Not The Playbook

By the end of week 4, you can name the exemption your raise will run under and explain why - to a co-founder, to an investor, and to your eventual securities counsel.

Reg D 506(b) versus 506(c) decision, Securities Act §4(a)(2) private-placement exemption, Investment Company Act §3(c)(1) versus §3(c)(7), accredited-investor verification logic, general-solicitation rules, Form D filing logic, blue-sky overlays, and Rule 506(d) bad-actor diligence. Live weekly call walks one real anonymised structure per session.

Pillar Two · Weeks 5-8

Build The Structure Before You Hire Counsel

By the end of week 8, you have a written structuring memo for your own fund - entity stack, Reg D path, fee model, governance - that your eventual counsel can build the actual paperwork from.

Entity stack (LLC fund, LP, fund-of-funds SPV, co-GP), carry and fee model design, capital-stack mechanics, sub doc + Op Agreement key terms, investor-deck sections that get read, broker-dealer line and issuer's exemption, SPV economics and when fund-of-funds is the right structure. Office hours weekly, 1:1 structuring review at week 8.

Pillar Three · Weeks 9-12

Run It Compliantly After You File

By the end of week 12, you have an ongoing-compliance binder for the structure you are running and a defined path back into RaiseLaw if you are ready to file your first or next fund.

Annual filings every fund owes, investor reporting cadence (regulation versus expectation), audit versus review, K-1 timing operations, regulatory-update tracking (SEC releases, no-action letters, enforcement notes), when to bring in a securities attorney mid-deal. Final week: "Ready-to-File" hot-seat for cohort grads ready to engage RaiseLaw for bespoke fund formation.

FAQ

Common questions, before applying.

How is this different from the Fund Founders Blueprint?

The Blueprint is a top-of-funnel capture - a PDF and a path. The Mentorship is the install version. 12 weeks of live weekly calls, office hours, 1:1 structuring review at week 8, a written structuring memo at the end, and a formal RaiseLaw handoff path at week 12 for grads ready to file. Same teacher, same regulation-grounded posture, deeper room.

Will you actually be on the live calls?

Yes. Every week, for the full 12 weeks. Weekly office hours too. The cohort is capped at 50 (25 per track) so the live time is real, not theatre.

Is this RaiseLaw?

No. RaiseLaw is the bespoke securities and real estate law firm for clients who want the actual filing, paperwork, ongoing counsel, and structuring done for them. The Mentorship is the cohort that teaches the aspiring or active fund founder how the regulations actually work - so when you sit down with counsel, the structure is already mapped and the engagement is faster, cleaner, and cheaper. Cohort grads ready to file get a formal RaiseLaw handoff path.

What is the time commitment?

3-4 hours per week for 12 weeks. One live weekly call. Weekly office hours (optional but encouraged). One 1:1 structuring review at week 8. Recordings if you cannot make a session live.

What is the price?

The Mentorship sits between the Fund Founders Blueprint and the RaiseLaw bespoke retainer. Two tracks: Aspiring Fund Founder and Active Syndicator. Both tracks, one-time enrolment. Exact pricing on the application.

Is this legal advice?

No. The cohort is education on securities law and capital-raise structure. It is not legal advice, not investment advice, and not an offer or solicitation to invest in any security. Personalised counsel happens through RaiseLaw under a separate engagement. The cohort is the education that gets you ready for that conversation.

Refund policy?

Yes. Finish the first two weeks, decide it is not for you, full refund within 14 days of cohort start. No questions.

Will the framework work for my entity / state?

The securities regulations are federal. The cohort is built on the Securities Act, Reg D, the Investment Company Act, and the relevant SEC releases - the same statutes that apply to your structure in California, Texas, Florida, or New York. State-specific blue-sky overlays are flagged as you reach them but are not the focus.

Why do I have to apply?

You do not have to. The application form is four minutes and helps me size cohorts and route the live time to where it is live in your raise. Submit, you get straight to enrolment - no setter calls, no back-and-forth.
